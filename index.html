Understanding Fire Science

Knowledge is the first step to fire safety. Understand how fire works to prevent disasters.

🔥 The Fire Triangle

Fire requires three elements: Heat, Fuel, and Oxygen. Remove any one element and fire cannot start or continue burning.

📈 Fire Behavior

Fire spreads rapidly, doubling in size every 30 seconds. It produces deadly smoke and gases before flames reach you. Escape is your priority.

🌡️ Temperature & Speed

Fires can reach 1000°F in minutes. Smoke and heat kill faster than flames. Most fire deaths occur from smoke inhalation, not burns.

⚡ Types of Fires

Class A: Wood, paper, cloth | Class B: Flammable liquids | Class C: Electrical | Class D: Metals | Class K: Cooking oils

Fire Prevention Tips

Most fires are preventable. Follow these essential safety guidelines.

🏠 Home Safety

  • ✓ Install smoke alarms on every level
  • ✓ Test alarms monthly
  • ✓ Replace batteries twice yearly
  • ✓ Keep flammables away from heat sources
  • ✓ Never leave cooking unattended
  • ✓ Keep electrical cords in good condition
  • ✓ Don't overload outlets

🍳 Kitchen Safety

  • ✓ Never leave cooking unattended
  • ✓ Keep flammable items away from stove
  • ✓ Wear tight-fitting clothes while cooking
  • ✓ Use oven mitts and pot holders
  • ✓ Keep a fire extinguisher nearby
  • ✓ If grease catches fire: cover with lid, turn off heat
  • ✓ Never use water on grease fires

🚬 Smoking Safety

  • ✓ Use deep, sturdy ashtrays
  • ✓ Never smoke in bed
  • ✓ Never smoke while drowsy
  • ✓ Fully extinguish cigarettes before disposing
  • ✓ Smoke outside when possible
  • ✓ Keep smoking materials away from children
  • ✓ Store lighters and matches safely

⚡ Electrical Safety

  • ✓ Have faulty wiring repaired professionally
  • ✓ Don't use extension cords permanently
  • ✓ Unplug appliances when not in use
  • ✓ Keep electrical cords away from water
  • ✓ Never touch electrical equipment with wet hands
  • ✓ Inspect cords for damage regularly
  • ✓ Use GFCI outlets in bathrooms and kitchens

🚭 What NOT To Do

  • ❌ Don't play with matches or lighters
  • ❌ Don't leave fires unattended
  • ❌ Don't burn trash or debris unsupervised
  • ❌ Don't use ovens to heat your home
  • ❌ Don't ignore smoke alarm warnings
  • ❌ Don't block fire exits
  • ❌ Don't start fires intentionally

📋 Emergency Escape Plan

  • ✓ Know two ways out of every room
  • ✓ Plan a meeting spot outside
  • ✓ Practice your escape plan
  • ✓ Keep exits clear of obstacles
  • ✓ Have a flashlight in each bedroom
  • ✓ Teach children to recognize smoke alarms
  • ✓ Get out fast - don't take possessions

Fire Department & Emergency Services

Professional firefighters are trained to respond to emergencies. Know how to contact them and cooperate effectively.

🚒 When to Call 911

Call 911 immediately if:

  • You see flames or heavy smoke
  • You smell gas or suspect a gas leak
  • Someone is injured or in danger
  • You need evacuation assistance
  • A building is on fire
  • There's an active fire hazard

📞 How to Call for Help

  1. Stay calm and dial 911
  2. Give your exact location/address
  3. Describe what's happening clearly
  4. Follow dispatcher instructions
  5. Stay on the line until told to hang up
  6. If you can't speak, stay on the line

🔔 Fire Department Services

  • Emergency response to fires
  • Medical emergency assistance
  • Hazardous material handling
  • Rescue operations
  • Fire safety inspections
  • Community education programs
  • Non-emergency assistance

🏥 During an Emergency

  • GET OUT IMMEDIATELY - don't stop for belongings
  • Stay low - crawl under smoke
  • Feel doors before opening - don't open hot doors
  • Go to your predetermined meeting spot
  • Call 911 from outside
  • Never return to a burning building
  • Help firefighters by staying out of their way

💡 Know Your Local Fire Department

Visit your local fire station, meet firefighters, and learn about community programs. Many offer free safety inspections and smoke alarm installations. Building a relationship with your local fire department helps everyone stay safer.

Wildfire Awareness & Safety

Wildfires spread rapidly and threaten entire communities. Preparation and awareness save lives.

🌲 Understanding Wildfires

Wildfires can spread at 14 mph or faster, destroying everything in their path. They produce massive smoke clouds, poor air quality, and can displace thousands. Climate change and drought conditions increase wildfire frequency and severity.

🚨 Evacuation Preparation

  • ✓ Know your local evacuation routes
  • ✓ Prepare an emergency kit (water, food, medications, documents)
  • ✓ Keep your car fueled (don't wait until evacuation is ordered)
  • ✓ Have insurance documents copies in fireproof safe
  • ✓ Create a digital photo inventory of your home
  • ✓ Keep important documents easily accessible
  • ✓ Have phone numbers for family members written down

🏠 Home Hardening

  • ✓ Clear gutters and roof of leaves/debris
  • ✓ Trim tree branches 6-10 feet from roof
  • ✓ Remove dead or dying trees
  • ✓ Space trees 10 feet apart minimum
  • ✓ Use fire-resistant roofing materials
  • ✓ Install metal mesh screens on vents
  • ✓ Box in eaves and overhangs
  • ✓ Mulch with non-flammable materials

⚠️ During Wildfire Season

  • ✓ Monitor air quality reports
  • ✓ Sign up for emergency alerts
  • ✓ Follow local fire department guidance
  • ✓ Wear N95 masks if smoke is heavy
  • ✓ Close windows and seal air leaks
  • ✓ Keep outdoor activities minimal on high fire days
  • ✓ Never ignore evacuation orders

🚭 Never Start Wildfires

Most wildfires are human-caused! Protect our forests and communities:

  • ❌ Don't discard cigarettes carelessly
  • ❌ Don't burn debris during fire season
  • ❌ Don't use charcoal grills in dry conditions
  • ❌ Don't leave campfires unattended
  • ❌ Don't intentionally set fires
  • ✓ Report suspicious fire activity to authorities
  • ✓ Support fire prevention programs

🌍 Climate Change & Wildfires

Rising temperatures and longer droughts create ideal conditions for wildfires. Support climate action, reduce carbon emissions, and advocate for forest management policies that protect communities. Everyone has a role in fire prevention.
